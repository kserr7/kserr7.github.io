Projects & Portfolio Artifacts

Far From Home

For my first video game ever, I chose a 2D pixel platformer. I learned the basics of game design, asset integration, Unity, C#, and pixel art. I used Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Animate to create the character sprites and animations. It was fun to see my art come to life through making an interactive character. The spritesheets for all of the movement animations were difficult but the most fun to make. The free tilemap I used is 2D Pixel Art Platformer | Biome - American Forest by Superposition Principle from the Unity Asset Store.

Pyromi Character Art

This is the main character of my game, Pyromi the fire dragon hatchling. I've got a short but solid level 1 with a simple point system, 'coin' collection in the form of forest fruit, and potential traps to avoid. I plan to continue developing Far From Home and potentially release it on a couple webbrowser game-sites for others to enjoy.

Arduino Unity Integration - "Ardity"

This is a screenshot of my work integrating an Arduino Micro controller component into my game. I used a rotary encoder to control the rising and falling of a floating platform, translating Arduino code to C# and finally into the game as a controller.

AR Experience

For my AR experience, I worked with a team of 4 other GIMM students to create an AR game using Unity and Vuforia, "Wizards vs Golems". I created an intro animation and the walk cycle animations for the enemy Golem characters. This was a great intro to AR, Adobe Animate, and Adobe Photoshop.

Avalanche!

"Avalanche!" is the current 3D game I'm working on using Unity. The project will continue on into the end of my junior year in the GIMM program. I've created a prototype of the playspace, character concept art, and the basic mechanics for the game in C#. This is my first time using the Terrain builder in Unity, and it was great practice with Adobe Photoshop for the character art. I've had a bit of experience creating 3D games in previous GIMM projects, but this one is the best looking one so far.
